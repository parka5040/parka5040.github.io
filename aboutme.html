About Me

I am a 23 year old Korean-American student from Purdue University who seeks opportunities to utilize my diverse skillset in the computational sciences to assist in decision making and data interpretation. I enjoy analyzing problems, coming up with creative solutions, and implementing them in a way that allows those around me to bring their own skills to the forefront.

My Background

I was originally born in Washington state. After moving around a few times across the contry, I moved to Indiana after I was accepted to Purdue University and I fell in love with the rural, quiet atmosphere. After joining the Indiana National Guard, I was committed to stay and incorporate myself as a Hoosier. I quickly settled down and now I have a wife and son.

I'm the child of two Korean immigrants and despite being born in the United States, I primarily speak Korean with my parents and immersed myself in the culture that they still held. In Washington, I grew around a lot of Korean-Americans who were in similar situations to myself culturally. However, after moving to Minnesota, and later Indiana, I was able to experience a diverse set of cultures from middle American to Indian and Arabic cultures. Due to my dual cultural and linguistic upbringing, I was really able to relate to and understand the cultural differences between us and embrace the value that they bring to both the workplace and my own life.

In my free time, I enjoy cooking for my family, gaming, reading, and following current events. Being in the National Guard, I also take enjoyment in exercising and physically improving myself as well when the time permits.
